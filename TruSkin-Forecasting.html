Definitions, Formulas & KPI Glossary. Every metric shown in this dashboard — with precise calculation methodology, worked examples using TruSkin Mar'26 data, and guidance on interpreting each number in the context of S&OP decision-making.
Demand Planning KPIs
Demand
Sales Forecast (SF)
Bottom-up unconstrained demand estimate. Reflects what the market will consume regardless of supply availability. Generated by the Sales/NAM team from POS data, promotions confirmed, and Nielsen category signals.

SF = Baseline + Promo Lift + Category Growth Adj.

Mar'26 example: Brand SF = 314,600 units = Σ(all retailer SF) × ASP $20.48 = $6.44M gross
Supply
Ops Plan (OP)
Supply-constrained shipment plan from Operations. Represents what can realistically ship given raw materials, production capacity, and DC inventory. In a supply-short scenario, OP < SF.

OP = min(SF, Constrained_Supply)

Mar'26 example: OP = 296,600 units — constrained by VCS1 L-Ascorbic Acid shortage (−12,400 units)
Planning Gap
S&OP Gap (Units & %)
The volume & revenue at risk when SF > OP. A positive gap means demand exceeds supply capability — revenue is potentially lost. A negative gap means excess supply risk (inventory build).

Gap Units = SF − OP
Gap % = (SF − OP) / SF × 100

Mar'26: 314,600 − 296,600 = 18,000 units = 5.7% gap = ~$0.37M at risk
Budget
Annual Operating Plan (AOP)
Pre-year committed revenue and volume budget set by Finance and Leadership. Incorporates prior-year actuals, Nielsen category growth outlook, new product launch assumptions, and strategic distribution targets. Fixed at year start; compared to rolling actuals monthly.

FY2026 AOP: ~3.49M units / ~$71.5M gross revenue
AOP assumption: +7.2% category growth (vs actual Nielsen Q1 +8.8%)
Forecast Accuracy Metrics
Accuracy
MAPE SF% — Mean Absolute % Error (Sales Forecast)
Measures average forecasting accuracy of the SF vs actuals, as a percentage. Lower = better. Calculated only over periods with confirmed actuals (Aug'25–Feb'26 = 7 periods).

MAPE SF% = (1/n) × Σ |Actual_t − SF_t| / Actual_t × 100
where n = number of completed actuals periods
Grading: A+ <2% · A 2–5% · B 5–10% · C 10–15% · D >15%
Aug'25 example: |252,800 − 257,400| / 252,800 = 1.82%
Brand 7-period MAPE SF ≈ 1.6% → Grade A+
Accuracy
MAPE OP% — Mean Absolute % Error (Ops Plan)
Same formula as MAPE SF% but using Ops Plan instead of SF. Typically slightly higher than MAPE SF because Ops Plan also absorbs supply-side uncertainty on top of demand uncertainty.

MAPE OP% = (1/n) × Σ |Actual_t − OP_t| / Actual_t × 100
Brand 7-period MAPE OP ≈ 2.0% → Grade A
Interpretation: A higher MAPE OP than MAPE SF signals supply-side execution gaps beyond pure demand error
Bias
Bias SF% — Systematic Forecast Direction
Measures whether the Sales Forecast consistently over- or under-predicts actual demand across periods. Unlike MAPE (which uses absolute values), Bias preserves direction so positive/negative errors do not cancel.

Bias SF% = Σ(SF_t − Actual_t) / Σ(Actual_t) × 100

Positive = SF over-forecasts (optimism bias)
Negative = SF under-forecasts (conservative bias)
Aug–Feb example: Σ SF = 1,790,600 · Σ Actual = 1,744,200
Bias = +46,400 / 1,744,200 × 100 = +2.7% (slight over-forecast)
Scenario
Scenario Bands — Bull / Base / Consensus / Bear
Forward-looking demand scenarios generated from the Base Sales Forecast to bound the planning range and inform inventory build decisions.

Bull = SF × 1.15 (all promos fire + category upside)
Base SF = statistical baseline + confirmed promos
Consensus= SF × 0.514 + OP × 0.486 (team-weighted)
Bear = SF × 0.84 (supply disruption + softening)
Revenue & Trade Finance
Revenue
Gross Revenue → Net Revenue → Gross Profit Waterfall
Three-step P&L waterfall from invoice revenue to operating profit contribution.

Gross Revenue = Units × ASP ($20.48)
Trade Spend = Gross Revenue × Trade Rate%
Net Revenue = Gross Revenue − Trade Spend
Gross Profit = Net Revenue × Gross Margin Rate%
Mar'26 brand example:
Gross: 314,600 × $20.48 = $6.44M
Trade (12.8%): −$0.82M → Net: $5.62M
Gross Profit (61.4%): $3.45M
Trade
Trade Rate by Retailer & Promo ROI
Trade rate = all customer-facing spend (slotting fees, TPR, display allowances, co-op ads, scan-downs) as % of gross revenue. Varies widely by channel model.

Amazon (AMS/PPC): 5.0% — no slotting
Target: 16.0% — display + TPR
Walmart: 18.0% — highest; TPR + scan-down
DTC: 2.0% — coupon/email only
Blended: 12.8%
Promo ROI = (Incr. Units × ASP) / Trade Spend
Target threshold: ≥ 2.5× for approval
Supply Chain Metrics
Inventory
Days of Supply (DOS)
How many forward days of demand can be covered by current DC on-hand inventory at the prevailing demand rate.

DOS = On-Hand Units ÷ (Monthly Demand ÷ 30)

≥ 14 days → Healthy (green)
8–13 days → Monitor (amber)
< 8 days → Critical (red) — stockout risk
Walmart Rogers DC, VCS1: 7 days → URGENT replenishment required. At current demand 8,200 units/month = 274 units/day, this DC runs dry in 7 days.
Replenishment
Replenishment Status Classification
Each DC replenishment status is classified based on DOS vs minimum safety stock target.

URGENT — DOS < 8d; order immediately
Triggered — DOS 8–13d; PO in transit
Pending — New item / planned launch
On Track — DOS ≥ 14d; no action needed
Safety stock target: 14 days = 2× average retailer lead time of 7 days from DC to shelf.
Syndicated Data KPIs (NielsenIQ + Profitero)
NielsenIQ
ACV% — All Commodity Volume Distribution
The percentage of total US retail store sales volume represented by stores that carry TruSkin. Weighted by store sales (not store count). A proxy for distribution reach — higher ACV = wider shelf presence relative to market volume.

ACV% = Σ(Sales of stores stocking SKU) / Σ(All store sales) × 100

TruSkin ACV: 73.6% (Q1'26)
Category leaders: ~90–95%
Gap = $8.2M untapped annual opportunity
Each +1pp ACV ≈ +188 new stores × 0.89 units/store/wk × 52 wks = +~8,700 units/yr ≈ $177K
NielsenIQ
Units per Store per Week
Units sold per stocking store per week — the CPG industry standard measure of physical sell-through velocity. Unlike $/TDP/wk, this metric is price-neutral and directly comparable across price tiers and pack sizes.

Units/Store/Wk = Total Units Sold / (Stocking Stores × Selling Weeks)

TruSkin Brand: 0.89 u/store/wk (+25.4% vs category)
Category avg: 0.71 u/store/wk
VCS1 hero SKU: 1.24 u/store/wk
Higher units/store/wk = faster turns → stronger retailer reset position, lower markdown risk, justifies deeper distribution investment.
Profitero
In-Stock Rate % & Demand Suppression Impact
The % of days/weeks a product shows as available on the digital shelf. Any in-stock rate below 100% means captured sales are lower than true unconstrained demand.

Unconstrained Demand = Captured Sales ÷ In-Stock Rate

Walmart VCS1: 72.4% in-stock
→ Lost demand = (1 − 0.724) × 8,200 = 2,263 units/mo
→ Revenue lost = 2,263 × $20.48 = ~$46K/month
Applied in S&OP: actuals adjusted upward to reflect true demand signal when in-stock <95%.
Profitero
Search Rank, Content Score & Price Compliance
Search Rank: Amazon keyword rank for category terms. Lower rank = better organic visibility. Rank improvement directly drives velocity uplift.
VCS1: #8 (Aug'25) → #3 (Mar'26) → +19% velocity gain
Each rank improvement pos 1–10 ≈ +3.8% organic velocity
Content Score (0–100): Listing quality score. Score <85 = −8% conversion penalty applied to forward SF model.
Price Compliance: Actual price / MAP × 100. Below 97% triggers trade finance alert.
Walmart 5 SKUs at 97.2% compliance = −$0.51/unit = $28K/mo MAP erosion
SKU & Channel Definitions
SKU Types
Single vs Bundle / Kit Classification
Single (S): One product in one pack. Revenue = 1 selling unit. Standard retail/e-commerce format. These are the primary demand-planning unit and have independent DC inventory and replenishment.

Bundle / Kit (B): 2+ individual products sold together as one SKU. Revenue is captured at the bundle MSRP — higher ASP than individual items. Bundles are primarily Amazon and DTC exclusive. Important notes:
• Bundle volume is NOT additive to component singles in demand planning (components are pulled for fulfillment)
• Bundle MAPE tracked separately — typically higher variance due to gift/seasonal demand patterns
• Bundle revenue ASP ranges $29.99–$59.99 vs single ASP $13–$36
